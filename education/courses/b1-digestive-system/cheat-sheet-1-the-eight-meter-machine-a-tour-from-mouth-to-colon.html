Cheat-Sheet · B1 Digestive System

Chapter 1 : The Eight-Meter Machine

One tube, two jobs (break food down, absorb it), five stations from mouth to colon.

The Five Stations

1. MouthChewing breaks food mechanically; saliva (with amylase) starts carb digestion and lubricates the bite.
2. EsophagusA muscular tube; peristalsis pushes the swallowed bolus to the stomach. No digestion happens here.
3. StomachHolds the meal, churns it, and adds acid (HCl) and pepsin; meters the result into the small intestine gradually.
4. Small intestineWhere most digestion and nearly all absorption happen, with help from bile and pancreatic enzymes.
5. Large intestineReclaims water and electrolytes, houses the microbiome, forms and stores stool.

Secretions & Sources

  • Salivary amylase : salivary glands; starts carbs.
  • HCl : stomach parietal cells; activates pepsin, defense.
  • Pepsin : stomach chief cells; starts protein.
  • Bile : made in liver, stored in gallbladder; emulsifies fat.
  • Pancreatic enzymes : pancreas; lipase, amylase, proteases.
  • Bicarbonate : pancreas; neutralizes stomach acid.

Movement

  • Peristalsis : wave that pushes contents forward.
  • Segmentation : mixes contents in place for absorption.
  • Migrating motor complex : the "housekeeper wave" that sweeps the gut clean between meals.
  • Cephalic phase : sight, smell, thought of food primes digestion before the first bite.

The Accessory Organs

  • Liver : the chemical plant; makes bile, processes nutrients.
  • Gallbladder : the holding tank; stores and releases bile.
  • Pancreas : the enzyme arsenal plus bicarbonate.

Why Surface Area Matters

  • Folds, villi, and microvilli multiply the small intestine's absorptive surface enormously.
  • More surface = more absorption. Lose that surface and absorption drops.
  • The gut wall also holds the enteric nervous system, the "brain in the wall."
Where education ends   This builds understanding of how the system works, not a license to diagnose or treat. Trouble swallowing, persistent pain, blood, or unexplained weight loss are signs that warrant evaluation by a qualified clinician. Work within your role, whatever it is.
