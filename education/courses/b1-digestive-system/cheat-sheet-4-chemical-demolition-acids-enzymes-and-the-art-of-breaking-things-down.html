Chapter 4 : Chemical Demolition

Chemical digestion is hydrolysis: enzymes use water to cut polymers into absorbable monomers. The right tool, in the right place, at the right pH.

The Enzymes Reference List

Salivary amylaseSalivary glands; cuts starch into maltose/oligosaccharides at mouth pH. Inactivated by stomach acid.
PepsinStomach chief cells (as pepsinogen, activated by HCl); cuts protein into peptides. Optimal pH ~1.5 to 3.5.
Pancreatic amylasePancreas; finishes starch into maltose/oligosaccharides in the alkaline duodenum. Does most carb work.
Trypsin / chymotrypsinPancreas (as zymogens); cut protein/peptides into smaller peptides. Alkaline optimum.
Pancreatic lipasePancreas; cuts triglycerides (after bile emulsifies them) into fatty acids + monoglycerides.
Brush border enzymesSmall intestine microvilli; disaccharidases (maltase, sucrase, lactase) + peptidases finish the last cuts where absorption begins.
Bile (not an enzyme)Liver / gallbladder; emulsifies fat, breaks NO bonds. Keeping it off the enzyme list tests whether you know emulsification from hydrolysis.

The Three Jobs of Acid

  • Denature protein : unfolds it so pepsin can reach buried bonds.
  • Activate pepsin : low pH converts pepsinogen to pepsin (autocatalysis). No acid, no pepsin.
  • Defend : kills most ingested microbes before they reach the gut.
  • Acid does NOT digest food directly. The goal is acid contained where it belongs, not less acid.

pH Is the Address System

  • Each enzyme has an optimal pH; outside it, the protein unfolds and activity collapses.
  • Pepsin works only in the acidic stomach; pancreatic enzymes only in the alkaline duodenum.
  • A swallowed enzyme in the wrong pH is a key in the wrong lock. Containing an enzyme is not delivering an active one.

Pancreas + Bile (the duodenum)

  • Bicarbonate : neutralizes gastric acid, raises pH so pancreatic enzymes can work. Called by secretin.
  • Enzyme cocktail : amylase, trypsin/chymotrypsin, lipase. Called by CCK (fat and protein).
  • Bile : emulsifies fat into tiny droplets so lipase has surface to work on; ferries products in micelles.
  • Fat is the strongest trigger for both enzymes and bile, so fatty meals test the system hardest.

When a Step Breaks

  • Reflux : acid in the wrong room (esophagus); a leaking valve + pressure, not overproduction.
  • Low acid (hypochlorhydria) : real but over-claimed; weakens all three acid jobs; linked to low iron and B12. Often caused by long-term acid suppression.
  • EPI : pancreas cannot deliver enough enzymes; hallmark is greasy, floating, foul stools (steatorrhea) + weight loss. Treated with prescribed enzyme replacement.
  • Lactose intolerance : one missing brush border enzyme; everything else digests fine.

The Worked Sequence (any meal)

  • Mouth : chewing + salivary amylase starts starch. Stomach : HCl denatures protein, activates pepsin, kills microbes; amylase paused; fat untouched.
  • Duodenum : secretin calls bicarbonate (neutralize); CCK calls enzymes + bile; enteropeptidase activates trypsin, which activates the rest; cocktail runs.
  • Brush border : disaccharidases + peptidases make the final cuts where absorption begins. Every dysfunction is a specific break in this exact line.
Coaching boundary   Teach how acid, enzymes, and bile work; refer the clinical stuff. Do not recommend, start, or stop acid-suppressing or enzyme products as treatment. Persistent reflux, suspected ulcers (pain, vomiting, black stools), greasy floating stools with weight loss, or any self-diagnosed "high acid / low acid" plan go to a qualified provider who can test, not a coaching plan.
