Part F · Reason About the Evidence

Honest teaching includes the limits. Each item asks for the reasoning, not a verdict.

  1. Someone says "the Stanford study proved fiber doesn't matter." Explain what the Wastyk trial actually showed, and why the diversity gain from fiber can lag (name the mechanism).
  2. Most of what is known about the microbiome is correlational. Explain why a correlation between low butyrate and a damaged barrier does not prove that low butyrate caused the damage, and name one reason the arrow can point both ways.
  3. "More probiotics means a healthier gut." Name the flaw in this claim (what happens to most swallowed strains) and the higher-leverage move you can actually point to.

Where education ends
This worksheet builds microbiome literacy, not a license to diagnose or treat, in yourself or anyone else. Understanding how fermentation and the butyrate engine work is general education; the signs that warrant medical evaluation (bloody stool, persistent pain, unexplained weight loss, or any alarm symptom) belong with a qualified clinician. Diagnosis and treatment of disease sit on the clinical side of that line, not in a supplement label. If you coach clients, that means you refer; a clinician works within their license; a self-learner does not self-diagnose.
