Chapter 6 : Named Conditions, IBS to IBD

One symptom can branch to many causes. Keep symptom, cause, and effect in separate columns, and sort signal from story.

The Five Conditions at a Glance

NameWhat it is (cause)Who diagnosesCoach's role
IBSFunctional, multi-mechanism gut-brain disorder (visceral hypersensitivity, altered motility); real pain, no tissue damage.Clinician, by Rome IV criteria after ruling out organic disease.Support an existing diagnosis; never call it "just IBS" yourself.
GERDMechanical: a weak or wrongly relaxing lower esophageal sphincter plus repeated acid exposure.Clinician; chronic or atypical cases need workup.Educate on boring, effective levers; refer reflux that wakes someone at night.
SIBOBacteria overgrowing a normally sparse small intestine, often downstream of impaired motility or certain drugs.Clinician; testing is shaky and over-applied.Split real biology from the overdiagnosis story; do not endorse breath-test labels or protocols.
IBD (Crohn's, UC)Immune-mediated inflammation that genuinely damages the bowel wall.Clinician, by colonoscopy plus biopsy, not symptoms.Recognize alarm features; refer promptly. Not yours to manage.
CeliacGluten-triggered autoimmune attack in HLA-DQ2/DQ8 carriers; flattens the villi.Clinician, by serology plus confirming biopsy (before gluten removal).Never suggest a diagnostic gluten elimination; testing comes first.

The Three Columns

  • Symptom : what the person experiences (bloating, reflux, pain). Branches to many causes.
  • Cause : the underlying mechanism. This is where the conditions actually separate.
  • Effect : the downstream consequence if the cause continues. This is where the stakes and scope live.
  • Same bloating on the surface can hide five different realities underneath.

"Leaky Gut," Split in Half

  • Real : increased intestinal permeability via loosened tight junctions, measurable, rises under stress, exercise, NSAIDs, and in IBD.
  • Inflated : the marketing story that it is the root cause of nearly everything, fixed by a supplement stack.
  • Often a consequence of inflammation, not the trigger. Association is not causation.

Alarm Features (Refer)

  • Blood in the stool.
  • Unintended weight loss.
  • Fever or nighttime symptoms that wake the person.
  • Anemia or unexplained iron deficiency.
  • Progressive or escalating pain.
  • These signal the effect column of an organic disease: stop coaching, refer.

Four Habits for Grading a Claim

  • Ask which column the claim is really making.
  • Separate association from causation.
  • Weigh the source and its incentives.
  • Notice what it leaves out (silence on red flags is itself a red flag).
Coaching boundary   These are clinical diagnoses; coaches educate and support, never diagnose or treat. Do not say someone "probably has IBS," do not recommend a diagnostic gluten elimination, and refer any alarm feature. When the effect column contains tissue damage, the situation belongs to medicine.
