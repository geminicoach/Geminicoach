Part C · Reason About the Evidence

Each item describes a real finding from the chapter. Answer with reasoning, not a single word.

  1. The Wastyk (2021) trial found that fermented foods raised diversity quickly while high fiber did not jump in 17 weeks. Explain why the authors did not conclude that fiber failed, naming the feature of fiber response that Fu et al. (2022) emphasize.
  2. The Zheng (2023) meta-analysis was positive AND still called for more high-quality trials. Explain to a client why those two statements are both true and not a contradiction.
  3. Mazzantini et al. (2021) found many probiotic products did not contain what the label claimed. Explain how this changes the way you would respond to a client who says "this probiotic isn't working," before you ever discuss whether the strain has evidence.
  4. Psyllium and inulin are both sold as "fiber" but do different jobs. Distinguish them by physical property (per McRorie and Fagerberg, 2017) and name which one is more likely to backfire in a sensitive, symptomatic gut and why.

Coaching boundary
Supplements are not treatments. This worksheet builds the skill of weighing claims and teaching the foundations, not a license to diagnose, treat, or prescribe. A coach educates on mechanism, points to food, water, movement, sleep, and stress first, advises checking with a provider before starting any supplement, and refers anything clinical (persistent symptoms, pain, blood, unexplained weight loss) to a qualified professional.
