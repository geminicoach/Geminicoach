Chapter 2 : The Hidden Ecosystem

Fiber feeds microbes, microbes make short-chain fatty acids, and butyrate fuels the colon lining.

The Core Pipeline

1. PlantsDeliver fermentable fiber your own enzymes cannot break down (inulin, pectin, resistant starch, beta-glucan).
2. ColonSlow, anaerobic, dense with bacteria: the fermentation headquarters where leftover fiber arrives.
3. MicrobesA cross-feeding assembly line breaks fiber down and ferments it. Diversity keeps the line staffed.
4. SCFAsOutput is acetate, propionate, butyrate in roughly 60:20:20. Both fuel and signal.
5. ColonocytesBurn butyrate for 70 to 80 percent of their energy, keeping the lining fed and sealed.

The Four Microbial Jobs

  • Finish digestion : ferment fiber your enzymes cannot, salvaging energy.
  • Make vitamins : K2 and some B vitamins (a contribution, not your main source).
  • Train immunity : Clostridia drive regulatory T cells toward tolerance.
  • Guard the surface : colonization resistance fills niches so pathogens cannot.

The Three SCFAs

  • Acetate : most abundant; peripheral energy, also feeds butyrate makers.
  • Propionate : taken up by the liver; gluconeogenesis, satiety, cholesterol.
  • Butyrate : stays local; primary colonocyte fuel and barrier builder.
  • Double duty : all act via FFAR2, FFAR3, GPR109A and as HDAC inhibitors.

Fiber by Fermentability

  • Fermentable : inulin, pectin, resistant starch, beta-glucan; feeds the engine.
  • Non-fermentable : wheat bran, tough skins; the broom that adds bulk and speeds transit.
  • Solubility is the old split; fermentability is what drives the biology.
  • A fiber supplement is not automatically microbiome food.

The Butyrate Loop

  • Virtuous : butyrate fuels colonocytes, they burn oxygen, lumen stays low-oxygen, anaerobes thrive, more butyrate.
  • Flipped : little fiber, less butyrate, underfed colonocytes, oxygen leaks in, butyrate makers lose ground.
  • Butyrate also seals tight junctions and supports mucus and immune tolerance.

Pre, Pro, Fermented

  • Prebiotic : food for the microbes you already have (fermentable fiber).
  • Probiotic : live microbes you swallow; mostly transient, rarely recolonize.
  • Fermented food : made by desired microbial growth; raised diversity in the Wastyk trial.
  • Higher leverage: feed the residents, build variety gradually.

Diversity, Stability, Resilience

  • Diversity : how many species, how evenly spread.
  • Stability : staying the same when undisturbed.
  • Resilience : bouncing back after a hit, via functional redundancy.
  • Many bakeries, not one: lose a species, another covers the job.

The Honest Limits

  • Most findings are correlational; direction of cause is often unclear.
  • Much mechanism comes from mice and germ-free models, not people.
  • Individual variation is large; there is no single ideal microbiome.
  • Be skeptical of stool tests that score you and sell the fix.
Where education ends   The mechanism (fiber to fermentation to butyrate) is general education; the clinical stuff is not. Bloody stool, persistent or severe pain, and unexplained weight loss are signs that warrant medical evaluation by a qualified clinician, not a supplement label. Work within your role, whatever it is.
