Where education ends
This worksheet builds digestive literacy, not a license to diagnose or treat, in yourself or anyone else. The in-scope work is understanding how acid, enzymes, and bile are supposed to work and recognizing what belongs with a clinician. Persistent reflux, suspected ulcers (pain, black stools, vomiting), the pancreatic-insufficiency pattern (greasy floating stools, weight loss), or any plan involving acid-suppressing or enzyme products warrants a qualified clinician. Starting or stopping acid-suppressing or enzyme products as a treatment is a clinical decision, not a general-education or self-care one. Work within your role, whatever it is.
