  3. A reaction looks allergic (flushing, headache) but no antibodies are involved. Explain how histamine intolerance can impersonate an allergy, and name the one enzyme whose shortfall lets it happen.

Coaching boundary
Allergy is a medical condition, not a coaching project. Diagnosis and management of true food allergy belong with a clinician, ideally an allergist. Never advise anyone to test a reaction by eating a suspected allergen; a supervised oral food challenge is done only in a medical setting with rescue treatment on hand. Throat tightening, widespread hives, or trouble breathing is a possible anaphylaxis emergency: call emergency services. Coaches build food literacy, support managing a confirmed, dose-dependent intolerance, and refer everything immune or uncertain.
