Chapter 5 : When the Machine Misfires

A symptom is the lamp at the end of the line, not the machine that broke. Read every signal backwards to its mechanism.

Read the Signal Backwards

Bloating / distensionNot a raw volume problem. Rides on three legs: fermentation load, gas transport and motility, and visceral sensitivity. Visible girth is loosely coupled to actual gas (abdominophrenic dyssynergia changes the container's shape).
Gas / belchingFlatus is mostly fermentation gas; troublesome belching is mostly swallowed air and a learned swallowing behavior, not gas made below.
ConstipationSlow transit pulls back too much water (hard, dry stool). Can be slow-transit, normal-transit, or a pelvic-floor / evacuation problem. Same complaint, three broken stages.
DiarrheaFast transit leaves too little time to reabsorb water, OR active secretion pours chloride and water into the lumen. Two different physiologies, not one.
Reflux / heartburnA barrier and motility failure (transient LES relaxations, weak tone, hiatal hernia), not acid overproduction. Acid is the irritant the leak exposes.
NauseaGenerated centrally in the brainstem as a protective alarm fed by many input lines (gut, balance, circulating chemicals, top-down). Same feeling, many triggers.
MalabsorptionSignal shows up far from the gut: fatigue, deficiencies, pale / greasy / floating stool. Distinguish maldigestion (breakdown fails) from malabsorption (uptake fails).

Transit-Time Logic

  • One organ, one variable. The colon manages water; transit time sets the output.
  • Slow : more water reabsorbed : hard, dry stool.
  • Fast : less water reabsorbed : loose, watery stool.
  • Bristol Stool Scale : Types 1-2 slow end, 3-5 normal middle, 6-7 fast end. A validated readout of transit, an observation tool, not a diagnosis.

Myths the Chapter Kills

  • "More gas = more bloating." Volume is a poor predictor; handling and sensitivity matter more.
  • "Heartburn = too much acid." Usually the barrier leaking, not the factory overflowing.
  • "Relief from antacids proves excess acid." It only proves acid is the irritant.
  • "Lots of gas means a broken gut." A dozen-plus times a day is normal fermentation.

What Normal Gas Looks Like

  • Passing flatus roughly a dozen to about twenty times a day is squarely normal.
  • Daily volume varies several-fold between healthy people and rises with fermentable fiber.
  • More gas on a high-fiber day is the microbiome doing its job, not a malfunction.
  • Belching after meals or fizzy drinks is ordinary plumbing.

Transient vs Persistent

  • Transient signal : one output from a system that is otherwise fine, with an identifiable input, then returns to baseline.
  • Persistent pattern : the same output repeating for weeks, or steadily changing from your baseline.
  • Frequency and duration are part of the symptom, not a detail about it.

Normal Signal vs Refer (Alarm Features)

Read as a signalBloating after a fermentable meal; gas a dozen-plus times a day; belching after meals; occasional heartburn after eating late; a transient change in stool with a clear cause; one bout of nausea with an obvious trigger; a single hard or loose stool.
Refer promptlyBlood (in stool, black tarry stools, blood or coffee-ground vomit); unexplained weight loss; anemia or its signs; trouble swallowing (food catches or hurts); persistent vomiting; nighttime symptoms that wake you; a sustained change in bowel habit; new symptoms after age 50 (or strong family history of GI cancer or IBD).
Coaching boundary   Understanding mechanisms makes you literate, not licensed. Read everyday signals as signals, but any alarm feature stops the mechanism conversation and starts the referral conversation. Alarm features lower the threshold for a professional to look; they are not a self-test, and nothing here is medical advice. Route to a qualified clinician.
