Chapter 9 Bonus : The Reaction You Can't See

One branch point (is the immune system involved?), three mechanisms, three completely different responses.

Allergy vs Intolerance vs Sensitivity

 AllergyIntoleranceSensitivity
Mechanism Immune-mediated. Body misreads a food protein as a threat (classically IgE: mast cells, histamine). No immune system. Missing or low enzyme, overloaded transporter, or a food chemical not cleared (e.g. lactase, DAO). Mixed bag. Some real (FODMAP, non-celiac wheat); mechanism often unproven or uncertain.
Typical signs Fast (minutes): hives, lip and face swelling, wheeze, vomiting; worst case anaphylaxis. Trace amount can trigger. Dose-dependent gut symptoms: bloating, gas, cramping, loose stool; flushing or headache for histamine type. Often vague and delayed: bloat, fatigue, fog. Real symptoms, weak or uncertain link to a specific food.
Who diagnoses An allergist (allergy and immunology specialist): skin-prick or food-specific IgE, read against real history; oral food challenge if unclear. Physician or gastroenterologist: hydrogen breath test (carbohydrate type) that should reproduce symptoms, not just gas. No single valid test. Structured elimination and reintroduction, ideally with a dietitian. IgG panels are not valid.
Coach role Refer. Build literacy, recognize red flags, never diagnose or test by eating it. Anaphylaxis is an emergency. Support management of a confirmed intolerance: thresholds, lower-lactose dairy, spacing, enzyme supplement. Educate on valid vs invalid testing; encourage proper evaluation; protect nutrition; refer to dietitian or physician.

The Branch Point

  • One question : is the immune system involved? That splits allergy (yes) from intolerance (no).
  • They are not points on a severity scale. Different mechanisms, not one running at different speeds.
  • Get it wrong and you either dismiss a deadly allergy or over-restrict a harmless reaction.

Lactose Intolerance

  • Lactase low : undigested lactose reaches the colon.
  • Symptoms = osmosis (water pulled in) + fermentation (bacteria make gas).
  • Dose-dependent: a splash is fine, a tall glass is not. Manage, do not eliminate.

Celiac : Its Own Category

  • Autoimmune, not allergy, not "sensitivity." Gluten triggers an attack on the gut's own tissue.
  • Measurable antibodies + villous atrophy; treatment is strict lifelong gluten avoidance.
  • Celiac trap : go gluten-free before testing and you can no longer diagnose it.

The Testing Trap

  • IgG panels measure exposure and tolerance, not disease. They flag your most-eaten foods.
  • Not endorsed by major allergy bodies. Hair, kinesiology, electrodermal all fail under blinding.
  • Valid tools: allergy = IgE / skin-prick; carb intolerance = hydrogen breath test.

Elimination as Experiment

  • Phase 1: rule out mimics first (celiac, IBD, infection) while still eating the suspect food.
  • Phase 2: remove one or two targeted foods for a defined window.
  • Phase 3 (the point): reintroduce one food at a time with washout. Removal alone proves nothing.
Coaching boundary   Allergy is a medical condition: refer to an allergist and never tell anyone to test a reaction by eating a suspected allergen. Throat tightening, hives, or trouble breathing is possible anaphylaxis, a call-emergency-services situation. Coaches teach mechanisms, support confirmed dose-dependent intolerances, and refer everything immune or uncertain.
