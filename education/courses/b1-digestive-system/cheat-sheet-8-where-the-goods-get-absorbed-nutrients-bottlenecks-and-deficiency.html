Chapter 8 : Where the Goods Get Absorbed

Digestion breaks food down; absorption is where it crosses the border and becomes you. Intake is not uptake.

The Address System: Who Crosses Where

DuodenumIron and calcium. Acid-dependent: stomach acid keeps non-heme iron soluble and reduced (Fe²⁺) for the DMT1 transporter.
JejunumThe workhorse: glucose and amino acids, folate (B9), and most water-soluble vitamins and vitamin C. Longest stretch, tallest villi.
Ileum (terminal)B12 (only here, only with intrinsic factor) and bile-salt recycling. No backup site for either.
ColonWater and electrolytes, plus short-chain fatty acids made when microbes ferment fiber. A second, microbe-driven absorption site.
Fat + A,D,E,KRide micelles (need bile) after lipase hydrolysis, packed into chylomicrons, exported via lymph. No fat in the meal, no micelle.

How the Crossing Works

  • Transcellular : through the enterocyte, usually via a specific transporter.
  • Paracellular : between cells, more passive (water, some minerals).
  • No transporter, no crossing : selectivity is also fragility.
  • Saturation : carriers overwhelm; smaller doses absorbed at a higher fraction.

Five Bottlenecks

  • Low stomach acid : iron + B12 suffer (different ZIP codes).
  • Flattened villi (celiac) : iron, folate, fat-sol., macros drop.
  • Rapid transit : less contact time; fat pathway hit hardest.
  • Missing lipase : fat + A,D,E,K to the stool (steatorrhea).
  • No bile : no micelles; fat + A,D,E,K strand at the door.

Most Deficiency-Prone, and Why

  • Iron : needs acid + intact duodenum (2 failure points).
  • B12 : acid + intrinsic factor + ileum, no backup (3 points).
  • A,D,E,K : lipase + bile + lining + transit (most links).
  • Folate : vulnerable when proximal villi are damaged.

Vitamin D Breaks the Rules

  • Largest source is sunlight on skin, not food or gut absorption.
  • It governs active calcium absorption: low D becomes low calcium and a bone problem.
  • Its dietary fraction still obeys all fat-absorption rules (A,D,E,K).
Where education ends   Understand the mechanisms and reason about meal composition (form and pairing of foods). A suspected deficiency needs lab confirmation and a clinician's care. Interpreting labs, deciding a number is "too low," diagnosing, or prescribing supplements as treatment require the relevant license, whatever your role. Neurological symptoms, fatty stools with weight loss, blood, or a flagged lab are signs that warrant medical evaluation by a qualified clinician.
