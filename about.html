Where it started
The Mat.

I was a wrestler in high school. Those years built something in me that never went away: a love of hard work, intensity, and pushing past what feels comfortable. After wrestling, I fell in love with lifting and never looked back. In college I started coaching conditioning for the volleyball and basketball programs, teaching athletes how to train with purpose and move with intention. I stayed on as an assistant volleyball coach focused on conditioning and weight training. Those early years confirmed what I already felt — watching someone unlock a physical capability they didn't know they had is one of the most rewarding things I've ever experienced.

The pivot
A Different Path.

I was originally headed toward chiropractic medicine. Bodybuilding redirected that curiosity toward digestive health and nutritional science, and I pursued additional coursework from there. I've continued my education since and try to stay current with the research as it comes out because the science never stops evolving. Along the way I've had the privilege of mentoring others in this field — something I genuinely love doing whenever the opportunity presents itself. I've done guest speaking for gym owners and been invited as a guest speaker at schools for anatomy and health sciences. Nearly ten years in, I'm still learning.

Who I work with
Everyone.

My clients run the full spectrum. The woman preparing for her wedding who wants to feel her best. The competitive bodybuilder dialing in their diet and stage condition. Everyone in between. No two programs are the same because no two people are the same.

What I Coach.

I compete. I don't just coach bodybuilders — I am one. I've stepped on the NPC stage myself and I understand firsthand what it takes to diet for a show, manage the physical and mental demands of contest prep, and peak at the right time.

That experience is something I bring directly into my coaching. When I'm guiding a client through prep, I'm not working from a textbook — I'm drawing on what I've lived through and continue to live through every time I compete.

This is also why I offer posing coaching as part of my training sessions. Stage presence, mandatory poses, and the details that separate a good physique from a great presentation — I've been on both sides of it.
